• Sputnik I

    October 4th, 1957 Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite was launched into orbit by the Soviet Union

  • Explorer VI Satellite Goes Into Orbit

    August 7th, 1959, the first photograph of Earth from orbit; they were taken by the U.S. satellite Explorer 6

  • Veterok and Ugolyok and Their 22 Days of Flight

    August 19th, 1960, first organisms (two dogs, a rabbit, 42 mice, and various species of plants and fungi) from Earth to return alive from space, they were launched into space aboard the Sputnik-5

  • Mercury Primate Capsule and Ham the Astrochimp

    January 31st 1961, first hominid launched into space, the chimpanzee Ham was sent to space aboard the MR-2 (Mercury-Redstone 2), returned alive after crashing into the Atlantic Ocean

  • Soviet Cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin

    April 12th, 1961, the first man in space, Yuri Gagarin was the first man to journey to outer space, became an international celebrity following his return to Earth

  • First mission Entering the Asteroid Belt

    July 15, 1972, the first spacecraft to leave the inner solar system sailed into the asteroid belt 45 years ago today, 15 July 1972, on a mission that would mark many firsts for NASA’s exploration of the solar system. Pioneer 10, the first outer solar system mission, became the first probe not only to leave the inner solar system, but also the first probe to be launched on an escape trajectory from the solar system and the first craft to visit the planet Jupiter.

  • The Apollo-Soyuz Mission

    July 15, 1975, the U.S. and USSR exhibited a rare moment of international collaboration and launched their first joint mission — the Apollo-Soyuz Test Project. The nine-day mission, which saw the docking of two spacecraft and included a famous handshake in space between U.S. astronaut Thomas P. Stafford and Soviet cosmonaut Alexei A. Leonov, was the culmination of years of talks, agreements and planning between the Soviet Academy of Science and NASA.
